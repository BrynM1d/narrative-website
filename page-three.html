The Site Today

The Site Today

The mine today is a lot different compared to when it first opened and makes the big trek up the Great Orme even more appealing. Not long after opening they realized the mine would be a popular attraction especially for tourists. So, they decided to open a gift shop selling a range of items from geodes, gemstones to souvenirs. The shop is so popular that some people come just to buy and not bother with the tour of the mines. Another part of the site that people come specifically for is the secondhand bookshop.

Visitors come in and sometimes donate unwanted books and the hardworking team sort them. Mostly the books are about history but it’s only £1 for a softback and £2 for a hardback so it’s definitely worth a look! However, the main attraction of course is the mine itself. For the self-guided tour it costs £9 for an adult, £6 for a child, £24.50 for a family ticket and under 5’s get in for free. The staff at the mine also do school groups which is where they get a lot of their revenue. When I worked there last year I helped take one of the school groups around and they really enjoyed it.
