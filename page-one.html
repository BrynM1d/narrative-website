The Discovery of the mines

  • 2009-2011

    The Great Orme mines started being mined roughly 4000 years ago and was worked on by bronze age miners for around 1000 years. It was found that the miners took stones from Llandudno beach and used animal bones to mine the ore and rock.

  • March 2011

    The mines were only discovered in 1987, this was due to a scheme to landscape part of the Great Orme. Once excavated archaeologists and mining engineers found the tunnels that dated back thousands of years ago. Through carbon dating which is a technique used on the stones and animal bones found. It was determined that the mine was active in the bronze age.

  • December 2015

    Since then over 30,000 bones have been discovered and the mine now has an entire room of stone hammers to show off to the public. Also, now that the mine had been excavated it was time to make sure it was safe for the public to walk through.
