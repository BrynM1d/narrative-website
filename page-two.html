The Bronze Age

The Bronze Age

The bronze age dates from around 3300BC to 1200BC and was the first time humans worked with metal. The bronze age comes after the stone age and instead of using stone tools we learnt to smelt copper ore to make bronze tools and weapons.

Smelting

So how did bronze age miners create bronze? Firstly, they had to mine for the copper ore and in the Great Orme mines’ case it was Malachite. Once this is collected it is crushed into dust then smelt down using pumps called bellows to increase the temperature of the fire. After some time, the fire and fuel source are left to cool then put in water. You will then see small copper nuggets or one large piece of copper depending on how much malachite was used.

However, this only makes copper and not bronze. To make bronze you need to smelt the copper again but this time in a crucible. Once melted you add tin which is another metal and would’ve been brought from a different mine. It is about 90% copper and 10% tin to make strong bronze that could be used as tools and weapons. Once melted together the liquid is poured into a mold and once cooled it is opened and voila you’ve made a bronze tool!
